Buttermilk Biscuits

Fluffy, layered, delicious buttermilk biscuits. Easy to make and freeze well, enjoy with butter and jelly or honey.

Ingredients

  • 1 stick of butter
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp baking powder
  • 2 1/2 cups flour (all purpose)
  • 1 cup and 3 tbsp buttermilk
  • 2 tsp honey or sugar (optional)

Note on buttermilk: cultured whole fat is ideal, but in a pinch any dairy milk with a couple tbsp of white vinegar mixed in and left to curdle for 10 minutes works as well.

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 425 °F
  2. Mix together dry ingredients in medium bowl
  3. Cut cold butter into half to quarter inch cubes
  4. Using your hands or a pastry cutter smush the butter into the dry mixture until there are little clumps the size of peas (over mixing is worse than under mixing)
  5. Pour one cup of buttermilk into the bowl with the dry buttery mixture and stir until just combined. Add more buttermilk as needed to get everything into one shaggy ball
  6. Dump onto counter and pat into a rectangle that is about an inch thick
  7. Cut the rectangle in half and stack two pieces on top of each other. Repeat process 3-4 times
  8. Cut biscuits into desired size/shape (I go for about 2 inch squares because it's easy)
  9. Place biscuits on parchment paper covered baking sheet and bake for 15 minutes or until tops are golden brown
